# ADR-003: Content Review Policy

## Status

Proposed

## Context

The resource library may publish templates, guides, checklists, sample letters, and documents for government-adjacent and public-service workflows.

Some content may be legal-adjacent, governance-adjacent, procurement-adjacent, or policy-sensitive. Examples include right-of-way agreements, permit-to-enter forms, consent documents, waivers, memoranda of agreement, and contract-style templates.

AI assistance can help draft and structure content, but public-facing content must be reviewed before publication.

## Decision

AI-assisted draft generation may be used for topic outlines, first drafts, metadata, slugs, internal linking suggestions, and template structure.

Human review is required before publishing any resource.

Extra review is required for legal-adjacent templates, agreement-style documents, consent forms, right-of-way documents, permit-to-enter templates, waivers, and compliance-related guidance.

Disclaimers must be used where templates could be mistaken for legal advice, official forms, mandatory language, or guaranteed compliance documents.

The project must avoid legal advice claims. Templates should be presented as general drafting references and should encourage adaptation according to office policies, applicable laws, and approval procedures.

## Consequences

This policy allows AI-assisted drafting while preserving human responsibility for accuracy, tone, risk management, and public-facing quality.

Content production may be slower for legal-adjacent templates, but the project will reduce the risk of publishing misleading or overconfident guidance.

Review requirements should be reflected in publishing workflows, task notes, and future content documentation.

## Alternatives Considered

### Fully Manual Content Creation

Lowest AI-related risk, but slower and less scalable.

### AI-Assisted Drafting with Human Review

Balances speed and quality. This is the recommended approach.

### Automated Publishing Without Human Review

Rejected for this project because sensitive templates and government-adjacent guidance require accuracy, disclaimers, and context-aware review.
