# Content Strategy: CiviCore Resource and Template Library

## 1. Project Content Purpose

The CiviCore Resource and Template Library will publish practical templates, guides, checklists, sample documents, and simple tools for local public-service workflows.

The content should help users complete common documentation, planning, digitalization, and coordination tasks faster while positioning CiviCore IT Solutions as a practical digitalization and systems partner for local institutions.

The first content phase should focus on free, useful, search-friendly resources. Monetization, downloadable packs, contextual SheetBot links, and interactive tools can be added later after the content base is established.

## 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Philippine LGU personnel
- Barangay officials and staff
- Water district staff
- Administrative personnel
- Planning and development office staff
- Small public-service organizations

Secondary audience:

- NGOs
- Cooperatives
- Small offices
- Community organizations
- Local project implementers

## 3. Initial Content Categories

### LGU Letters

Formal letter templates for common local government and public-service office needs, including requests, endorsements, transmittals, invitations, certifications, and memoranda.

### Project Proposal Templates

Structured proposal templates for barangay, LGU, water system, street lighting, office improvement, and local infrastructure projects.

### Right-of-Way / Permit-to-Enter Templates

Reference templates for property access, temporary entry, easement coordination, right-of-way agreements, and consent documents.

These are legal-adjacent resources and must include disclaimers and extra review before publication.

### Water District Resources

Guides, checklists, public advisory templates, service forms, customer service resources, and digital modernization planning materials for water districts.

### Office Digitalization Guides

Practical guides for file organization, Google Workspace setup, shared drive structures, records management, office cybersecurity, and website/content readiness.

### Simple Calculators/Tools

Simple calculators, checklists, estimators, and recommendation tools that help users plan costs, organize documents, assess readiness, or choose templates.

## 6. Standard Content Page Format

Each resource page should follow a consistent structure:

- Title
- Slug
- Category
- Tags
- Meta title
- Meta description
- Introduction
- When to use this resource
- Template/guide body
- Notes for customization
- Disclaimer if needed
- Related resources

Recommended slug examples:

- /lgu-request-letter-template
- /barangay-project-proposal-template
- /permit-to-enter-private-property-template
- /water-district-website-modernization-checklist

## 7. SEO Positioning

Content should be practical, plain-language, and search-friendly.

SEO principles:

- Use plain language.
- Use Philippine local government context where appropriate.
- Use search-friendly headings.
- Create one useful page per template or guide.
- Avoid thin, duplicate, or filler content.
- Include clear meta titles and meta descriptions.
- Use internal links between related templates and guides.
- Prioritize mobile-friendly page structure.
- Keep public-facing content useful even before monetization.

## 8. Content Review Rule

AI may assist in drafting outlines, first drafts, metadata, slugs, related resource suggestions, and template structure.

Human review is required before publication.

Legal-adjacent templates require extra review and a disclaimer. This includes right-of-way agreements, permit-to-enter forms, consent documents, waivers, agreement-style templates, and compliance-sensitive guidance.

Default disclaimer pattern:

```text
This template is provided for general reference and drafting assistance only. Users should review and adapt it according to their office policies, applicable laws, and approval procedures.
```

Content must not claim to provide legal advice, official legal sufficiency, mandatory wording, or guaranteed compliance.
