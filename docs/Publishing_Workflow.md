# Publishing Workflow: CiviCore Resource and Template Library

## 1. Purpose

This document defines the publishing workflow for the CiviCore Resource and Template Library.

The workflow is designed to support useful public resources, consistent editorial quality, search-friendly publishing, and careful review of legal-adjacent or compliance-related content.

The project follows the current AIOS workflow with ChatGPT and Codex only. ChatGPT supports planning, review, documentation, and strategic guidance. Codex implements approved repository and documentation tasks.

## 2. Content Workflow

### Topic Queue

Maintain a topic queue for templates, guides, checklists, calculators, and future downloadable resources.

Each topic should include:

- Working title
- Category
- Target audience
- Search intent
- Priority
- Review risk level
- Related resources

### AI-Assisted Draft Generation

AI may assist with outlines, first drafts, page structure, metadata ideas, slug suggestions, and related resource suggestions.

AI-assisted drafts must be treated as drafts only and must not be published without human review.

### Human Review

Every resource must be reviewed before publication.

Review should check:

- Accuracy
- Usefulness
- Tone
- Formatting
- Local government context
- SEO metadata
- Disclaimers where needed
- Risk of being mistaken for legal advice or official guidance

### SEO Metadata Preparation

Each resource should include:

- Page title
- Slug
- Category
- Tags
- Meta title
- Meta description
- Related resources

### Formatting

Resources should be formatted for easy scanning and reuse.

Use clear headings, short sections, copy-friendly template text, practical notes, and mobile-friendly structure.

### Scheduled Publishing

Approved resources may be scheduled for publication to maintain a consistent publishing rhythm.

Scheduling should prioritize the launch batch first, then continue with related resources that support internal linking and category growth.

### Sitemap Update

After publication, the sitemap should be updated or regenerated through the selected publishing platform.

For WordPress v1, sitemap updates may be handled by WordPress core or an SEO plugin.

### Post-Publication Review

Published content should be reviewed after launch for:

- Formatting issues
- Broken links
- Search performance
- User usefulness
- Outdated details
- Missing disclaimers
- Opportunities for internal links

## 3. Content Status Lifecycle

Use the following lifecycle for resource pages:

- IDEA
- DRAFTING
- REVIEW
- APPROVED
- SCHEDULED
- PUBLISHED
- NEEDS_UPDATE
- RETIRED

Status definitions:

- IDEA: Topic is identified but not yet drafted.
- DRAFTING: Draft content is being created.
- REVIEW: Draft is ready for human review.
- APPROVED: Content is approved for publication.
- SCHEDULED: Content is scheduled for a future publication date.
- PUBLISHED: Content is live.
- NEEDS_UPDATE: Content is live but requires revision.
- RETIRED: Content should no longer be publicly promoted or should be removed/redirected.

## 4. Review Rules

General guides require standard review for accuracy, usefulness, tone, formatting, and SEO readiness.

Legal-adjacent templates require extra review. This includes right-of-way agreements, permit-to-enter forms, consent documents, waivers, contracts, MOAs, MOUs, and similar documents.

Compliance-related content must be written cautiously. It should avoid claims of guaranteed compliance, official approval, or legal sufficiency.

No content should claim to be official legal advice.

Templates should include disclaimers where users may mistake the content for legal, official, mandatory, or compliance-guaranteed guidance.

## 5. Automation Boundaries

AI may:

- Draft content
- Suggest metadata
- Suggest related links
- Suggest slugs
- Suggest categories and tags
- Suggest content refresh opportunities

AI may not:

- Publish content without review
- Create legal certainty claims
- Claim a template is official or legally sufficient
- Generate direct outreach campaigns
- Bypass human review for legal-adjacent templates
- Publish procurement, compliance, or legal guidance without extra review

## 6. WordPress Publishing Notes

WordPress is the accepted v1 platform for the resource library.

Publishing rules:

- Use drafts first.
- Use categories and tags consistently.
- Use scheduled posts when possible.
- Maintain clear slugs.
- Add disclaimers where required.
- Use a consistent content page format.
- Keep titles and headings search-friendly.
- Avoid unnecessary plugins.
- Review SEO metadata before publishing.
- Confirm sitemap behavior before public launch.
